Background Information

Family Life

Born on February 9th, 1773 in Berkeley at his family's plantation in Virginia. William Henry Harrison was the youngest of seven kids. His parent were Benjamin Harrison V and Elizabeth Bassett Harrison. He grew up very wealthy.

Education

Harrison received a classical education at Hampden-Sydney College in Virginia, where he was a student. He then studied medicine in Richmond, Virginia, and in Philadelphia. But, the death of his father caused Harrison to stop his studies.

Accomplishments Before Election

Harrison enlisted in the U.S. Army in 1791, serving in conflicts with Native American tribes in the Northwest Territory. He gained leadership experience and national recognition, starting as an ensign in the 10th Regiment at Fort Washington near Cincinnati. By 1792, he was promoted to lieutenant.
